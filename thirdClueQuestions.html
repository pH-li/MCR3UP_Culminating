Crashing Waves

The height of waves that crash onto the side of a boat relative to sea level can be represented by a sinusoidal function. The waves reach a max height of 2 meters above sea level before falling down to a height of 0.5 meters above sea level. The waves take 3 seconds to fall from its maximum height to its minimum height. When time is at 0 seconds, the wave is at its maximum height.

Note: Work with RADIANS.

a) Write a function to represent the height of the wave above sea level in meters (h) as a function of time in seconds (t). Write the function using cosine. (2 marks)

b) How long is the function's period? (1 mark)

c) Determine the AROC between t = 50 seconds and t = 55 seconds. (2 marks)

d) When is the first time the wave not falling or rising? Do NOT consider the time at 0 seconds. (2 marks)

e) Approximately what is the IROC at t = 24.5 seconds? Make h equivalent to 0.01.
